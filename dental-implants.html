Biocompatible Tooth Replacement

A dental implant is a titanium post surgically positioned into the jawbone beneath the gum line to act as a stable, permanent root for your replacement teeth. Because they fuse directly to your jawbone, implants provide rock-solid support for crowns, bridges, and dentures — allowing you to speak, smile, and eat with complete confidence.

Why Choose Dental Implants?

Unlike conventional bridges or dentures, implants do not require adjacent teeth to be ground down or altered. They help preserve your natural facial structure by preventing the bone loss and resorption that naturally occurs after tooth extraction. Implants are a lifetime solution that looks, feels, and functions exactly like your natural teeth.

What is a Dental Implant?

A dental implant consists of a biocompatible titanium post placed in the jawbone and a custom crown fixed on top. Implants are highly versatile and used for:

Single Tooth Replacement

One implant and one custom crown to replace a single missing tooth without affecting neighboring teeth.

Multiple Missing Teeth

An implant-supported dental bridge to restore multiple missing teeth stably and comfortably.

Full-Mouth Restoration

An implant-supported full arch (All-on-4/6 concept) to restore the entire smile for patients missing all teeth.

Precision Technology & Planning

Getting a dental implant right starts with an accurate mapping of your jawbone — that's where guesswork and potential complications are eliminated. At Dr. Karpe's clinic, every implant case follows a precise two-scan protocol:

3D CBCT Imaging

Before surgery, a detailed 3D CBCT scan maps nerves, sinuses, and bone density. Our team coordinates this scan at a trusted partner imaging center and reviews the results to plan a safe, precise implant position.

  • Safe, precise implant mapping
  • CBCT scan coordinated for you
  • Zero guesswork before surgery

iTero Lumina Scanning

For your crown and bite alignment, we use the advanced iTero Lumina intraoral scanner to capture highly detailed 3D digital impressions — avoiding uncomfortable and messy putty trays.

  • Mess-free 3D digital impressions
  • Crowns designed to match your natural bite
  • View your teeth scans live on-screen

Your Treatment Journey

Our implant team follows a consistent, proven process for every patient, from first scan to final crown, ensuring highly predictable clinical outcomes.

1

Consultation & Scan

Our specialists examine your oral health and take an iTero Lumina scan to assess your candidacy.

2

3D CBCT Imaging

We coordinate a 3D CBCT scan to map out bone density and crucial structures (nerves/sinuses) for safe placement.

3

Treatment Planning

Our clinical team reviews both scans to finalize your plan: exact implant count, positioning, and custom restoration design.

4

Implant Placement

The implant is placed under local anesthesia. Most patients report only mild, manageable post-op discomfort.

5

Healing Period (3 to 6 Months)

The implant fuses securely with your jawbone (osseointegration) over 3 to 6 months to create a permanent tooth root replacement.

6

Custom Crown Placement

Your custom-designed crown is fitted to match your natural bite and color, completing your new tooth.
