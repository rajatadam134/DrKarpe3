How To Save Your Childs Teeth?

  1. Pediatric Care
  2. June 23, 2026
  3. Article

Maintaining the health of milk teeth is just as important as caring for permanent teeth. Milk teeth are essential for proper nutrition, speaking, and they hold the correct spaces in the jaw for permanent teeth to erupt later. If a milk tooth is lost prematurely due to decay, the adjacent teeth can shift, leading to crowding and orthodontic issues when permanent teeth arrive.

Establish Early Brushing Habits

Brushing should start as soon as the first baby tooth appears. For infants, wipe gums with a clean, damp cloth. For toddlers, use a soft-bristled toothbrush with a smear (rice-grain size) of fluoride toothpaste. Teach your child to brush twice a day – in the morning and right before bed. Make brushing a fun activity by using timers or songs!

Monitor Diet and Sugary Snacks

Sticky sweets, chocolates, sodas, and packed fruit juices are major culprits behind childhood cavities. Bacteria in the mouth feed on sugars to produce acids that attack enamel. Limit sugary snacks to mealtimes rather than constant grazing, and encourage drinking plenty of water after snacks to rinse away food debris.

Preventative Clinical Treatments

We recommend several highly effective preventive treatments performed at our clinic:

  • Professional Fluoride Application: A safe, quick gel or varnish applied by our dentist to strengthen your child's developing enamel and reverse early micro-decay.
  • Dental Sealants: Thin protective coatings painted onto the chewing surfaces of the back molars to block out food particles and cavity-causing plaque.
  • Pediatric Crowns (Pedo Crowns): For teeth with extensive decay, these restore shape and prevent premature tooth loss.

By following these basic guidelines and scheduling regular checkups every six months, you can ensure your child grows up with a cavity-free, healthy, and confident smile.
