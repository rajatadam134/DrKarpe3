What Is Next After Tooth Removal?

  1. Restorative Dentistry
  2. June 18, 2026
  3. Article

Losing a permanent tooth is more than just an aesthetic issue. When a tooth is removed and not replaced, it initiates a series of shifts in your mouth. The adjacent teeth begin to tilt and drift into the empty space, disrupting your bite and causing alignment issues. Furthermore, the opposing tooth in the other jaw can start to over-erupt (grow longer) into the gap.

Over time, the jawbone in the extraction area starts to degrade (bone resorption) due to lack of chewing stimulation, which can eventually lead to facial sagging and premature aging. Can we replace missing teeth? Yes, absolutely! Modern restorative dentistry offers highly comfortable and functional replacement options to match your natural teeth perfectly.

1. Removable Dentures

These are traditional appliances that can be taken out and cleaned by the patient:

  • Removable Partial Dentures (RPD): Replaces one or more missing teeth, supported by a metal or plastic framework that clips onto your remaining natural teeth.
  • Complete Dentures: Replaces an entire arch of missing teeth (upper, lower, or both), resting directly on the gums.

2. Fixed Prosthetics

These are permanently anchored and do not need to be removed for cleaning:

  • Fixed Dental Bridge: A bridge replaces missing teeth by using the adjacent natural teeth as anchors. The neighboring teeth are prepared and fitted with crowns that support the artificial tooth in the middle. We use premium metal-free zirconia materials to ensure bridges look fully natural and offer maximum strength.
  • Advanced Dental Implants: The modern standard of tooth replacement. A biocompatible titanium post is surgically anchored directly into the jawbone, acting as a new tooth root to support a single crown, bridge, or denture.

Why Implants Are Highly Recommended

Choosing an implant-supported tooth provides several key advantages: it prevents jawbone resorption, does not require shaping or grinding down adjacent healthy teeth, and feels and functions exactly like your original tooth.
