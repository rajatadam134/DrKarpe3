Get It Done Or It Will Be Too Late!

  1. Oral Hygiene
  2. June 12, 2026
  3. Article

Teeth are an integral part of the body. It is very important to take care of your teeth to prevent any damage.

A tooth is covered by a natural protective layer called “ENAMEL”. Enamel is the hardest tissue in the human body, but once it gets destroyed, it cannot be regenerated. It gets damaged permanently. Therefore, it is absolutely vital to get any damage treated before it spreads deeper into the inner tissues of the tooth i.e. the DENTIN and the PULP.

"Early management of a damaged tooth is highly beneficial. Neglecting minor decay can lead to the infection progressing deep into the root tissues, causing severe pain and structural loss."

Preventive Dental Fillings

One should check their teeth in the mirror regularly for any black spots or decay. As soon as you notice any discoloration or black spots on your teeth, visit your dentist immediately.

If a tooth is initially decayed, it can be treated quickly and painlessly by filling the decayed cavity with a modern tooth-colored composite filling material. This is the right time to get it done!

What Happens If You Neglect It?

If a black spot or cavity is neglected, the decay progresses deeper, damaging the inner tissues of your teeth. This leads to pain and sensitivity to hot and cold food items. At this stage, a simple filling is no longer sufficient, and you will require a Root Canal Therapy (RCT) to clean out the infection and save the tooth, followed by a protective crown.

If still neglected, the tooth will eventually break or decay completely, leaving no other option but to perform a surgical extraction to remove the root piece and avoid spreading infection into the jawbone.

Therefore, early detection and treatment not only save your natural teeth but also save you from dental pain and expensive treatments. Don't delay – get your minor cavities filled today!
